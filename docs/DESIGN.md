# CommerceKit Design System

## Brand Direction

CommerceKit should feel like a warm, premium commerce operations product: practical, tangible, and business-focused. It should not look like a generic SaaS dashboard or a simple product grid.

## Visual Principles

- Show product depth through catalog, checkout, order, and operations layers.
- Use warm retail tones with a deep operational green.
- Keep card hierarchy clear and avoid nested decorative cards.
- Use visible calculations and order states as proof of product logic.
- Keep interactions subtle and responsive.

## Colors

- Background: `#FBF7EF`
- Foreground: `#1B1B18`
- Primary: `#2E3F34`
- Accent: `#B76935`
- Secondary: `#F1D59B`
- Muted: `#EFE6D6`
- Border: `#DED0B9`

## Components

- Navbar: compact, public product links, FR/EN switch, checkout CTA.
- Hero: product cockpit rather than a static illustration.
- Product card: category, color block, inventory, cents-based price.
- Checkout: customer form plus transparent order summary.
- Order page: public confirmation with token and status.
- Dashboard: operations metrics, order queue, revenue visibility.

## Responsive Rules

- Mobile-first layout with single-column product and checkout flows.
- Desktop hero uses product copy beside an operations mockup.
- Tables collapse into simple grid rows where possible.

## Accessibility Rules

- Labels are required on forms.
- Buttons use semantic links or submit buttons.
- Text contrast stays strong against warm surfaces.
- Heading order stays page-local and readable.

## Anti-Patterns To Avoid

- Generic purple gradients.
- Product cards without business data.
- Decorative dashboards that do not show real workflow.
- Low-contrast beige text.
- Too many badges with no meaning.
